9	Brand Identity	Brand Guidelines 2025
Our brand identity is the most prominent element of
our brand; it identifies and distinguishes the brand in
people’s minds.
This section outlines the intent behind the brand
meaning and brand usage. All brand identity elements
and rules are to be followed to maintain a consistent
brand look.

-- 9 of 32 --

The StraitsX logo is designed
to reflect the brand’s
personality and offering.
The logomark, inspired by the
concept of “Interwoven Connections”,
represents seamless integration and the
unification of systems into a cohesive
network with dynamic curves that
convey motion and adaptability.
The wordmark is bold and modern, with
rounded edges and forward-slanting
cuts that balance professionalism,
progression and approachability.
Together, these elements create a
logo that encapsulates the essence of
connectivity, innovation, and security.
Logo

We hold our brand logo with
tremendous value, and thus it
should not be mistreated. To
maintain consistency, do not use
it in the following ways or in any
other way that is not approved
in this guideline.
The same rules apply to all logo
versions and the brand symbol.
DO NOT rotate or change the axis of the lockup.
DO NOT skew, warp, or distort in any way.
DO NOT alter the scale of the individual logo elements,
including the trademark symbols, or the spacing and
their relationship to each other.
DO NOT take out any elements within the logo.
DO NOT change the colour profile or adjust
the colour combinations.
Ensure that the logo is always visible and sits on the
correct background colour and that the correct logo
version is in use.
DO NOT modify any elements within the logo.
DO NOT add a drop shadow.
DO NOT crop the logo. Exceptions apply when using the
brand symbol as a design feature or visual anchor in
the artwork.

Red Hat Display Bold
Primary
Typography
Red Hat Display is a modern
geometric sans-serif with strong
legibility. Its clean lines, balanced
proportions, and distinctive
angled cuts—such as the angled
upward slope on the ‘t’—mirror
the wordmark, reinforcing the
brand’s modern professionalism
and consistency. It is a web-safe
typeface that comes with a variety
of weights and is designed for
legibility in user interfaces.
Red Hat Display is used for
headlines, subheads, and attention-
grabbing copy, while numerals are
set in Red Hat Mono.
The typeface family is available
for download from Google Fonts.
