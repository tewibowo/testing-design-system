# Reference Desk

> A curated set of resources from across the web. Seminal essays, talks, books, or other works that shaped how the author and a few admired others think about design.

_Source: interfacecraft.dev/library — collection: reference-desk_

---

## 1. The Princess Rescuing Application“This essay (by Daniel Cook) illuminates just how much current approaches to software design are completely at odds with human creativity, what truly motivates us, and how we learn through play and exploration. It's been 15 years since I first read it, yet it feels like we're still catching up to it.”Andy Allen

**Link:** https://lostgardenhome.files.wordpress.com/2019/09/mixing_games_and_applications.pdf

---

## 2. Inventing on PrincipleThis is probably the single greatest talk I've ever seen. It fundamentally changed how I think about the role of creation.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NGYGl_xxfXA

---

## 3. You Have Not Yet Heard Your Favourite Song“This book taught me that falling in love with one of your interests (like music) will allow you to fall in love with all of your other interests more deeply.”Gabbi Soong

**Link:** https://www.amazon.com/Have-Heard-Your-Favourite-Song/dp/191448715X

---

## 4. Magic Ink: Information Software and the Graphical InterfaceA dense but rewarding essay on information design. Bret Victor argues that most software is information software, and the key challenge is showing users the right information at the right time.

**Link:** http://worrydream.com/MagicInk/

---

## 5. What 'The Best' Looks Like“A sharp essay on the gap between good and great. Alex Kurilin articulates what most people only feel intuitively.”Rauno Freiberg

**Link:** https://www.kuril.in/blog/what-the-best-looks-like/

---

## 6. A Brief Rant on the Future of Interaction DesignA short, punchy critique of our industry's obsession with touchscreens. Bret Victor reminds us that our hands are capable of so much more than poking at glass.

**Link:** http://worrydream.com/ABriefRantOnTheFutureOfInteractionDesign/

---

## 7. First of Kind“First of Kind is the story of software design told through conversations with its pioneers. Soleio speaks to designers working at the very frontier of emerging technologies.”Soleio

**Link:** https://firstofkind.com

---

## 8. Learnable ProgrammingAn exploration of how programming environments can be designed to support learning. The principles here apply far beyond just teaching code.

**Link:** http://worrydream.com/LearnableProgramming/

---

## 9. The Power of Beauty in Communicating Complex Ideas“Beauty isn't decoration—it's a vehicle for understanding. This piece makes the case that aesthetics and clarity are the same thing.”Rauno Freiberg

**Link:** https://www.doc.cc/articles/power-of-beauty

---

## 10. What Screens WantFrank Chimero's meditation on the nature of screens and digital design. He argues for embracing the unique qualities of the medium rather than fighting them.

**Link:** https://frankchimero.com/blog/2013/what-screens-want/

---

## 11. The Web Hasn't Happened Yet“The greatest web design talk you've never seen.”Rauno Freiberg

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GadLRCdViPU

---

## 12. Dive Club“There's no one with their finger on the pulse more than Ridd. His interviews on Dive Club are wide reaching and unbelievably insightful. The way he interviews people feels like you're sitting on a couch or at a dinner table with them. Authentic and educational. Two of my favorite things in one.”Mike Smith

**Link:** https://www.dive.club/

---

## 13. When We BuildA beautiful and emotional talk from my friend Wilson Minor on the importance of taking care in what we create and considering the impact it has.

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i2FPU7bKD9k

---

## 14. Design Q&A, Charles Eames (1972)“This timeless gem beautifully expresses the soul of design in a way that is so very Eames.”Gentry Underwood

**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bmgxDCujTUw&t=14s
