## Overview

The persistent left navigation in the authenticated product, providing access to top-level destinations and account context, available in Business, Personal, and Sandbox variants.

---

## Structure

1. **Sidebar container** — The outermost shell. Pinned to the left edge for full viewport height. The Sandbox variant uses a distinct background treatment to signal a non-production environment.
2. **Brand logo** — Brand lockup pinned at the top. The sub-label differs per variant: "Business Account", "Personal Account", or "Sandbox". Always visible.
3. **Company display** — Shows the active company name and account type. Not present on Personal accounts. See Behavior for how this differs between single-company and multi-company Business accounts.
4. **Sidebar items** — Top-level nav items with icon and label. The active item is highlighted with a filled background. Items can carry a "New" badge and an expand chevron when they contain sub-navigation.
5. **Sidebar sub-items** — Second-level items indented under their parent when expanded. No icon. The active sub-item uses the same filled background as a top-level active item.
6. **Regulatory badge** — Pinned to the bottom. Displays MAS licensing text and a shield icon. Always visible; does not scroll with nav content.

## Behavior

### Parent item types

Nav items at the parent level have two distinct behaviours, determined per item by product content:

- **Expand-only** — Clicking opens and closes the sub-item list. The item has no route of its own and is never active. Example: "Treasury."
- **Navigable** — Clicking navigates to its own route and also expands the sub-item list. Can carry an active state when the current route matches it directly and no sub-item is selected. Example: "Home."

### Active state rules

- Exactly one item — a standalone nav item, navigable parent, or sub-item — is active at a time.
- Active state is driven by the current URL route, not by click alone. A click that does not result in a successful route change must not trigger the active state.
- When a sub-item is active, its parent row carries no active fill. The parent remains expanded as a wayfinding cue only — it never carries an active fill while a child is active.
- An expand-only parent is never active, regardless of which sub-item is selected beneath it.

### Expand / collapse

- Clicking an expand-only or navigable parent toggles the sub-item list open and closed.
- Only one group is expanded at a time. Opening a new group collapses any previously open group (accordion behaviour).

### Text truncation

Nav item labels that exceed the available width are truncated with an ellipsis. No wrapping.

### Company Switcher dropdown

- Opens as an overlaid panel to the **right** of the trigger button, top-aligned, listing available companies with a checkmark on the active selection, a divider, and account-level actions.
- Dismisses on outside click or Escape key.

## Guidelines

### When to use

- When the product has 3 or more top-level destinations navigated frequently within a session.
- When nav items carry sub-items that benefit from hierarchical visibility without leaving the current page.
- When account and company context must remain persistently visible — especially in multi-company environments.

### When not to use

- Do not use for single-page utility flows such as onboarding, setup wizards, or checkout — use a top nav or stepper instead.
- Do not use for settings-only surfaces where a tabbed layout provides better spatial hierarchy.
- Do not render the Sidebar in unauthenticated views (login, forgot password, sign-up).
- Do not add account-level settings (e.g. "My Profile", "Billing Plan") into the Sidebar nav — those belong in the Top Navigation account dropdown.

## Placement

### Desktop (≥ 1280px)

- Fixed to the left edge of the authenticated viewport. Does not scroll with page content.
- Width is fixed at the defined desktop value. No collapsed/icon-only mode in the current MVP.
- `z-index` sits above main content but below modal overlays, drawers, and toast notifications.
- Always paired with the Top Navigation bar.

### Mobile (< 1280px)

- Moves entirely off-canvas in the resting state. Not visible.
- Navigation is accessed through a hamburger icon in the Top Navigation bar.
- Tapping the hamburger opens the Sidebar as a left-edge drawer overlay, sliding in over the content without pushing it.
- A scrim covers the content area behind the open drawer. Tapping the scrim or selecting a nav item dismisses the drawer.
- The Company Switcher dropdown opens in-place within the drawer.
- The MAS Badge remains anchored to the bottom of the drawer regardless of nav content length.
- The nav container scrolls inside the drawer if content exceeds the visible drawer height.
